01The Story新闻事实

Touch 'n Go opened an Enhanced RFID lane at Malaysia's AKLEH Dato Keramat Toll Plaza. Existing RFID tags remain compatible. The company plans 10 enhanced lanes by December 31, 2026.Touch 'n Go在马来西亚AKLEH拿督克拉末收费站启用增强型RFID车道。现有RFID标签可继续使用。该公司计划在2026年12月31日前部署10条增强型车道。

  • Titan Flow combines dual RFID antennas, ANPR, AI and machine learning.Titan Flow结合双RFID天线、车牌自动识别、人工智能和机器学习。
  • Touch 'n Go claims 99.98% vehicle-detection accuracy in real-world conditions.Touch 'n Go称，该系统在实际环境中的车辆检测准确率达99.98%。
  • The upgraded lane supports RFID tags first issued in 2018.升级车道兼容2018年首发的RFID标签。
  • The lane is designed to support future barrier-free MLFF tolling.该车道旨在支持未来无栏杆的多车道自由通行收费。
¶

The AKLEH lane tests a higher-redundancy RFID system within Malaysia's existing toll network, while nationwide MLFF standards and deployment remain unresolved.AKLEH车道在马来西亚既有收费网络中测试更高冗余的RFID系统，但全国MLFF标准和部署仍未落定。

Key takeaway核心结论

The AKLEH rollout adds verification layers without requiring new tags, but it is a limited deployment ahead of Malaysia's broader MLFF decisions.AKLEH部署无需更换标签并增加核验层级，但在马来西亚更广泛MLFF决策前，仍属有限部署。
